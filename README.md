Early detection of diseases affecting tree leaves plays a crucial role in maintaining plant health, supporting ecological balance, and ensuring sustainable forestry practices. This project presents an automated system for tree leaf disease detection that leverages the YOLO (You Only Look Once) algorithm, a state-of-the-art object detection model, to analyze high-resolution images of tree leaves. The system is designed to classify leaves as healthy or infected by specific diseases, providing real-time results directly on an LCD display. Data preprocessing techniques, including resizing, normalization, and augmentation, are applied to enhance the model’s ability to generalize across various tree species and disease types. By automating the segmentation and feature extraction process, the system eliminates the need for manual inspection and reduces errors associated with conventional methods, ensuring accurate and efficient disease identification in diverse environmental conditions. In addition to disease detection, the system integrates a recommendation module that delivers tailored fertilizer suggestions through SMS alerts, providing actionable treatment guidance to farmers, arborists, and environmentalists. This approach bridges the gap between disease identification and management by offering timely intervention strategies without requiring specialized expertise in plant pathology. The performance of the model is evaluated using standard metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 score to ensure reliability and robustness. By combining advanced detection technology with practical treatment recommendations, the system contributes to improved tree health monitoring, sustainable agricultural practices, and enhanced ecosystem preservation.
